Containers vs Virtual Machines

A Virtual Machine is like building a whole house — with its own plumbing, wiring, and foundation. A Container is like renting an apartment — you get your own private room, but everyone shares the building's plumbing (the OS kernel). Watch the difference in action.

🏠 The House Analogy
Each VM is a separate house. Every house has its own foundation (hardware emulation), plumbing (OS), electrical (drivers), and rooms (apps). Safe — if one house burns, others are fine. But building a house takes time and resources.
🏢 The Apartment Analogy
Each container is an apartment in a shared building. The building provides plumbing (OS kernel), electrical (drivers), and structure. Each tenant gets their own private room with a locked door (namespace isolation), but shares the infrastructure. Faster and cheaper to move in.
