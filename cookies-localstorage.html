Cookies & LocalStorage

When you log in, choose dark mode, or add to cart — how does the website remember after you refresh? It uses tiny data stores in your browser: Cookies (tickets for the server), LocalStorage (your personal notebook), and SessionStorage (a sticky note you throw away when you leave).

🍪 Cookie = Ticket Stub
The store (server) stamps a ticket and gives it to you. Every time you come back, you automatically show the ticket. The cashier reads it and says "Ah, welcome back!"
📓 LocalStorage = Personal Notebook
You write notes in your own notebook that lives in your bag. The store never sees it — but you can check it anytime to remember your preferences. It never gets thrown away.
📋 SessionStorage = Sticky Note
You jot something on a sticky note while you're in the store. The moment you walk out (close the tab), the sticky note goes in the trash.
