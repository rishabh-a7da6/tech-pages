Symmetric vs Asymmetric Encryption

Symmetric encryption uses one shared key — like two people with copies of the same house key. Asymmetric uses a public/private pair — like a mailbox anyone can drop letters into, but only you can open. Watch both in action.

🔑 The Shared Key Analogy
Alice and Bob both have a copy of the same house key. Alice locks the box with her key, sends it to Bob, and Bob unlocks it with his identical key. Problem: How did they share the key in the first place without someone intercepting it?
📬 The Mailbox Analogy
Bob puts a mailbox on his front lawn — anyone can drop a letter in through the slot (encrypt with public key). But only Bob has the key to open the back and read the letters (decrypt with private key). No shared secret needed!
