The Tor Network (Onion Routing)

Tor provides anonymity by wrapping your data in multiple layers of encryption (like an onion) and bouncing it through a relay circuit. At each stop, one layer is peeled off. No single node knows both who you are and where you are going.

The Tor Guarantee: The chain deliberately breaks the link between identity and destination. To de-anonymize a user, an attacker would have to control BOTH the specific Entry Node and Exit Node being used simultaneously.
📖 Analogies & Concepts
📬 The Nested Envelopes:
Imagine putting a letter in an envelope addressed to Japan. You put that envelope inside another one addressed to Germany. You put that one inside a final envelope addressed to France.

You hand the stack to France. France opens the outer envelope, sees "Send to Germany," and forwards it. Germany opens it, sees "Send to Japan." Japan opens it, finds the final letter and delivers it to the website.

No one along the way saw the whole picture!
